SPREADING NATIVE PLANTS

As a New Jerseyians, I value our local ecosystem. I want to understand the complex intertwining effects of plants, insects, birds, us humans, to even mushrooms. As a fragile little bubble, Earth is an extremely diverse planet, composed of billions of different species. At EUG we are reviving the values of biodiversity through native plant gardens.

PLANTS & BIODIVERSITY

Plants are the roots of extremely diverse ecosystems. The Amazon, for example, host up to 80000 plant species, which then host a believed 2.5 million insect species. The rainforest is one of the most biodiverse places in the world, having new species discovered every year.

In setting where native plants, or plants in general, are absent, the results are an extreme lack of biodiversity. A suburban lawn typically consist of 3-5 non-native plants including large chunks of high maintain grass. A lawn like this generally supports 5-10 species of insect, with a portion being non-native insects.

Native wildlife struggle to adapt to a non-native plants diet. Their bodies to not digest the non-native leaves as efficiently as native leave. This starts to have a butterfly effect on the rest of the ecosystem. If the non-native insects thrive, the bird, amphibians, and other small animals will also have digestive issues with the non-natives compared to the natives.

HOW YOU CAN HELP

☉ Start a garden ☉ Include native plants in your garden ☉ Decrease grass areas ☉ Increase wildflower areas ☉ Put up a bird feeder ☉ Put out a bird bath ☉ Talk to friends about biodiversity

Keeping a variety of plants in your garden increases biodiversity. Biodiversity is all about supporting as many species as possible. Having a diverse garden full of mints, milkweeds, and laurels support exponential insects.

Milkweeds for example come in an assortment of varities, Common, Butterfly, and Swamp, all producing beautiful flowers. Milkweed is the host plant for the endangered Monarch Butterfly, meaning milkweed is the only type of plant these butterflies will lay their eggs on.
